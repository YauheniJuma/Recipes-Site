Puri

In Georgia, bread is called puri (pronounced “poo-ree”) and is traditionally baked in a deep circular clay oven called a tone (pronounced “ton-AY”). Traditional bread, especially the long pointed bread called shotis puri, is very popular and usually served with every meal.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

1 h.

serving

4 servings

Tags : bread

instructions

step 1

Pour 2 kg of flour into a large bowl. Make a hole in the flour and pour 700 ml of warm water into it. Add yeast and salt. Stir the ingredients until they are mixed well.

step 2

Now, knead the dough for 5 minutes, and put it back in the bowl. Cover the top of the dough with cellophane and set aside.

step 3

Now, put a warm cloth on it and let the dough rest for 30 minutes, and put it in a warm place like an oven while the Shotis Puri is resting; put firewood, salami or pine in the oven.

step 4

At this stage, preheat the oven, knead the dough with floured hands again, and leave for another 10-15 minutes. Remove Shotis Puri out of the oven before baking. Shape them into the desired size. You can give it an oblong shape.

ingredients

2 kg flour

700 ml warm water

20 grams yeast

30 grams salt

tools

Large Bowl

Cellophane

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups

Oven
